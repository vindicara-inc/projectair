# Video Scripts — Vindicara / Project AIR™

Two scripts for two different programs. Different length, different format, different tone.

**Record both in the same session.** Your wardrobe, lighting, and framing will be consistent; you'll warm up your delivery on one before landing the other cleanly.

---

## 1. Techstars video — 2 minutes

**Format:** founder-to-camera, production-quality tolerated (title cards, basic edit), shoulder-up framing, clean background. ~315 words at natural pace.

### Script

**[0:00–0:12] Hook.** Camera on you. Calm, eye contact.

> Hi, I'm Kevin Minn, founder of Vindicara.
>
> Every week I talk to security teams who just deployed AI agents into production. The same question comes up: **when something goes wrong, can we prove what happened?** And the answer, almost always, is no.

**[0:12–0:30] Problem.** Slightly slower.

> Prompt injection, tool misuse, rogue agents sending the wrong wire or leaking the wrong file — the incidents are real, and they're happening now. Prevention tools try to stop them. But when an incident reaches your legal team, your regulator, or your insurance carrier, you don't need a prevention log. You need evidence.

**[0:30–1:00] Solution.** Slight lean forward.

> That's what I built. Project AIR. It writes a cryptographically signed forensic record of every action an AI agent takes — signed with Ed25519, hashed with BLAKE3, chained forward. Tamper with any byte, and verification breaks at the exact step.
>
> As of today, Project AIR ships all ten OWASP Top 10 for Agentic Applications detectors, plus an EU AI Act Article 72 evidence generator, under MIT license. `pip install projectair`. We're the first open-source reference implementation of that full taxonomy.

**[1:00–1:30] Traction.** Concrete. Specific numbers.

> In the last three weeks: version 0.3.0 live on PyPI, 200 tests passing, public admissibility architecture documented at vindicara.io/admissibility, signed-chain integrity mapped to US Federal Rules of Evidence and EU eIDAS. I'm warming design-partner conversations now, ahead of a May 4 Hacker News launch.

**[1:30–1:50] Why me.**

> I've been building security tooling for years. This category didn't exist six months ago, and somebody has to build it before the EU AI Act enforcement deadline in August. That's why I went full-time on Vindicara.

**[1:50–2:00] Ask.** Steady. No ask-voice.

> Techstars has the operator network. I need the reps. I'd like to be in the next class.

### Delivery notes

- Wardrobe: plain dark shirt. No branded merch. Clean low-busy background.
- Framing: shoulders-up. Eye line directly into the lens.
- Pace: ~155 wpm (breathable).
- Memorize hook (first 12 sec) and ask (last 10 sec). Middle can be on teleprompter.
- Record 3 takes minimum.
- Title card (0.5 sec): "Kevin Minn · Vindicara / Project AIR™"
- Outro card (0.5 sec): "vindicara.io · pip install projectair"
- Muted music optional, -20 dB. Skip if uncertain.

## 2. YC video — 1 minute

**Format:** minimalist. Direct-to-camera, no production. **Polish reads as a negative signal.** Webcam is correct. ~210 words at quick pace.

### Script

**[0:00–0:12] Introduction.** Straight to camera.

> Hi YC, I'm Kevin Minn, solo founder of Vindicara.
>
> I know you prefer co-founders. I'm going to address that in twenty seconds. But first, here's what I'm building.

**[0:12–0:35] What you're building.** Specific, concrete.

> Project AIR is a cryptographically signed forensic layer for AI agents. Every action an agent takes is recorded, signed with Ed25519, chained together. When something goes wrong — and with autonomous agents, something will go wrong — I produce an evidence record a court, a regulator, and an insurance carrier can verify.
>
> Shipped today: ten of ten OWASP Top 10 for Agentic Applications detectors, MIT licensed, on PyPI. First open-source reference implementation.

**[0:35–0:50] Why solo.**

> Solo is by design for the first six months. I'm shipping code, sales, docs, and the pitch. This is a product that requires security instinct and speed. I can move faster alone than waiting for a co-founder to align. I'll add a technical co-founder when we have paying customers and I can recruit from a position of traction.

**[0:50–1:00] Close.**

> The EU AI Act enforcement deadline is August second. I'm building the evidence layer that makes compliance possible. I want to be in your summer batch.
>
> Thanks for watching.

### Delivery notes

- **Record on laptop webcam.** Not DSLR. Not studio lighting. The DIY look is correct.
- Look straight at the camera. Don't drift to a second monitor.
- Pace ~200 wpm. Fast but clear.
- Plain solid-color shirt. No company merch.
- **No intro card. No outro card. No music. No titles.** Just you talking.
- Try for one take. First-take authenticity beats third-take polish.
- Upload: .mp4, <200MB, direct to YC application form.

## Side-by-side cheat sheet

| Element | Techstars (2 min) | YC (1 min) |
|---|---|---|
| Camera | Good webcam or DSLR, fixed | Laptop webcam only |
| Edit | Title cards OK, basic cuts OK | No edit. One take preferred. |
| Music | Optional, muted | None |
| Pace | 155 wpm | 200 wpm |
| Open | "Hi, I'm Kevin Minn, founder..." | "Hi YC, I'm Kevin Minn, solo founder..." |
| Solo note | Not addressed (Techstars accepts solos) | **Addressed at 0:00–0:12** (YC prefers teams) |
| Ask | "I'd like to be in the next class." | "I want to be in your summer batch." |
| Outro card | Yes | No |

## Record both in the same session

Order: record the YC (shorter, harder) first when you're freshest. Then record Techstars (longer, more forgiving). Same wardrobe, same lighting, same background.

**Do not shoot both in the same outfit in a single take** — if your shirt has a wrinkle or your hair is off in one, it'll show up in both. Freshen between takes.

## Things to NOT say in either video

- Dollar amounts or valuation
- Competitor names (Lakera, Guardrails, CalypsoAI, etc.)
- Other accelerator names
- "Revolutionary," "10x," "leader," "disruptive"
- Names of OWASP individuals
- Specific customer names unless they've approved
- Anything you wouldn't defend under questioning

## After recording

- Watch both yourself on half-speed; catch verbal tics.
- Have one other person watch each version and give 30-second feedback.
- Don't over-iterate. Third or fourth take is usually the one.
- Upload. Submit. Move on. The video is <10% of the application; the written content is ~70%.
